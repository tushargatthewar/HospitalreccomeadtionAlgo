Mukhyamantri Amrutum Yojana Scheme

The Government of Gujarat has launched the Mukhyamantri Amrutum (MA) Yojana to alleviate the financial strain on households, particularly those below the poverty line, caused by soaring healthcare costs. This scheme aims to provide access to quality medical and surgical care through a network of healthcare providers, offering essential support and reducing the risk of catastrophic health expenses for vulnerable families.Learn More

Mamta Taruni Abhiyan

The Mamta Taruni Abhiyan program in Gujarat offered free medical check-ups, iron and folic acid supplements, and deworming medication to adolescent girls aged 10 to 19 and pregnant women. It also aimed to raise awareness about menstrual hygiene, family planning, and nutrition. Implemented by health workers including ASHAs, ANMs, and Anganwadi workers, this initiative played a crucial role in enhancing maternal and child health in rural areas of GujaratLearn More

Bal Sakha Yojana Scheme

The Bal Sakha Yojana is a community-driven effort aimed at safeguarding the well-being of vulnerable children, managed by the Department of Women and Child Development in collaboration with NHM and the State Child Protection Society. Trained volunteers, or "Bal Sakhis," are deployed to identify and report cases of child abuse, neglect, and exploitation, while also extending support to families in need, with a focus on children aged 0-18 from disadvantaged backgrounds.Learn More
